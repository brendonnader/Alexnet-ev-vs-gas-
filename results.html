Training Curves

Loss Over Epochs

Figure 1: Training and validation loss across 10 epochs. The curves fluctuate, which suggests the model is having trouble finding a stable minimum, likely due to the small dataset and noisy gradients.

Accuracy Over Epochs

Figure 2: Training accuracy peaks at about 75.9%, while validation accuracy stays between roughly 42% and 57%. This gap shows that the model fits the training data better than the validation data and does not generalize very well.

Confusion Matrix

Figure 3: Confusion matrix on the test set. The model predicted all cars as Gas: 83 Gas cars were correctly classified, but 112 Electric cars were all misclassified as Gas. This leads to a final test accuracy of 42.56% and shows that the model completely fails to recognize the Electric class.

Incorrect Predictions

Figure 5: Examples where Electric vehicles were incorrectly classified as Gas. Many of these cars look visually similar to gas sedans, which makes the problem harder. It also confirms that the model is heavily biased toward the Gas label.

Key Takeaways

  • The final test accuracy is 42.56%, which is better than random guessing, but not good enough for real-world use.
  • The model predicts only the Gas class, so recall for Electric cars is 0%.
  • This behavior suggests class imbalance, limited training data, and the need for more tuning (more epochs, balanced dataset, stronger data augmentation).
  • Even though the performance is limited, the project still demonstrates the complete pipeline of using AlexNet: data loading, preprocessing, training, evaluation, and visualizing results.
