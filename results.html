Results & Visualizations

Loss Over Epochs

Figure 1: Training and validation loss across 10 epochs. The curves fluctuate, which suggests the model is having trouble finding a stable minimum, likely due to the small dataset and noisy gradients.

Accuracy Over Epochs

Figure 2: Training accuracy peaks at about 75.9%, while validation accuracy stays between roughly 42% and 57%. This gap shows that the model fits the training data better than the validation data and does not generalize very well.

Confusion Matrix

Figure 3: Confusion matrix on the test set. The model predicted all cars as Gas: 83 Gas cars were correctly classified, but 112 Electric cars were all misclassified as Gas. This leads to a final test accuracy of 42.56% and shows that the model completely fails to recognize the Electric class.

Incorrect Predictions

Figure 5: Examples where Electric vehicles were incorrectly classified as Gas. Many of these cars look visually similar to gas sedans, and the model is heavily biased toward the Gas label.

Key Takeaways

  • Final test accuracy is 42.56%. The model performs better than random, but is not reliable enough for real-world deployment.
  • The confusion matrix shows 0 Electric cars correctly classified and 83 Gas cars correct – the model always predicts Gas.
  • This behavior suggests dataset issues (size/imbalance) and the need for more tuning: more Electric images, more epochs, and stronger augmentation.
  • Even with limitations, the project demonstrates the full deep learning pipeline: data preprocessing, AlexNet fine-tuning, evaluation, and visualization.
